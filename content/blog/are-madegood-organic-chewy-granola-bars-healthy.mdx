# Are MadeGood Organic Chewy Granola Bars Healthy?

## Quick Answer
MadeGood Organic Chewy Granola Bars can be a relatively healthy snack depending on your goals. They are light in calories and made with simple ingredients, but they may not be filling enough for everyone.

## Nutrition Snapshot
MadeGood Organic Chewy Granola Bars offer a balanced profile for a snack. They are light and made with clean ingredients, which is appealing for many consumers. 
- Calories per serving: 100
- Protein g: 2
- Sugar g: 6
- Sodium mg: 10
- Overall score: 7/10

## What Makes MadeGood Organic Chewy Granola Bars a Good Choice
- Light with only 100 calories, making it a sensible choice for a quick snack.
- Clean and simple ingredients, like organic oats and vegetables, suggest a focus on quality.
- Low sodium content at 10mg, which is good for a light snack.

## Potential Downsides
- Moderate protein at 2g, which might not be enough for everyone seeking satiety from a snack.
- 6g of total sugar, which is moderate but something to consider if you're watching sugar intake.

## How MadeGood Organic Chewy Granola Bars Fit Different Goals
- **Weight loss / calorie control:** Good fit if you need a low-calorie snack option, as they contain only 100 calories per bar.
- **High protein / muscle support:** Less ideal if you're looking for a high-protein snack, given the moderate protein content of 2g.
- **Energy / satiety / blood sugar stability:** Watch out for potential hunger soon after eating, as 2g of protein and 6g of sugar may not provide lasting energy.
- **Heart health / lower sodium / less processed eating:** A good choice for lower sodium needs, with only 10mg per serving.

## Healthier or Better Alternatives
- Plain Greek yogurt: Higher in protein, which can enhance satiety.
- Hard-boiled egg: More filling per calorie, offering better protein content.
- Whole orange: Less added sugar and provides fiber for better blood sugar stability.
- Raw nuts: Higher in healthy fats and protein compared to MadeGood Organic Chewy Granola Bars.
- Air-popped popcorn: More filling per calorie, offering a lower calorie density.
