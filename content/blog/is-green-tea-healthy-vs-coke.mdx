# Is Green Tea Healthy? (Green Tea vs Coke)

## Quick Answer
Green Tea is not particularly healthy, scoring a 5/10 due to its high sugar content of 33.5g, similar to Coke's 25.5g. Both options may not provide lasting satiety.

## Quick Verdict
- Green Tea: 5/10
- Coke: 4/10

Winner: Green Tea — slightly better due to its hydrating properties despite the high sugar content.

## Why Green Tea Falls Short
- Contains 33.5g of sugar, typical for sweetened beverages, which may not provide lasting satiety.
- Lacks protein and fiber, reducing its ability to satisfy hunger as a snack.
- Provides only a minimal 5g sugar hit, which might not be enough for energy.

## How Coke Compares
- Coke has 25.5g of sugar, leading to a quick energy spike without lasting fullness.
- Contains 105 calories of liquid, which may not fill you up as effectively as solid snacks.
- Both drinks offer moderate calorie content, with Coke at 105 calories compared to Green Tea's 115 calories.

## Best Choice Based on Your Goal
- **Weight loss:** Neither — both are high in sugar and low in satiety.
- **Energy / satiety:** Neither — both options provide quick energy without lasting fullness.
- **Occasional treat:** Green Tea — offers hydration but still high in sugar.

## Better Alternatives
- Sparkling water with a splash of lemon — zero calories and no sugar.
- Herbal tea — naturally caffeine-free and sugar-free.
- Plain iced green tea — refreshing without added sugars.
- Fresh fruit — provides natural sugars with fiber for fullness.
- Whole nuts — high in protein and healthy fats to keep you satisfied.
