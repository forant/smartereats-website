# Are Garden Veggie Straws Healthy? (Garden Veggie Straws vs Doritos)

Garden Veggie Straws often bask in a health halo, marketed as a better snack option compared to traditional chips. But are they really the upgrade you think? Despite their claims of being a lighter choice, the nutritional facts tell a more nuanced story that might surprise you.

**Short answer:** Not really — Garden Veggie Straws have a high sodium content and lack filling nutrients, making them less healthy than you might expect.

## The quick verdict
Garden Veggie Straws scored 6/10, which is on par with Doritos, also scoring 6/10. While both snacks might not be the healthiest, they reveal trade-offs in their own ways.

## What hurts Garden Veggie Straws
Garden Veggie Straws are high in sodium at 880 mg, which is quite significant for a snack that is marketed as light. Additionally, they barely contain protein (0.21g) and fiber (0.29g), making them less satisfying and potentially leading to overeating.

## Why the comparison matters
Doritos, while often criticized as ultra-processed junk, provide 10g of protein — a stark contrast to the nearly negligible protein in Garden Veggie Straws. This comparison highlights how the health halo surrounding veggie straws can be misleading, as their score reflects the lack of nutritional density in contrast to the perceived health benefits.

## Bottom line
Garden Veggie Straws may seem like a healthier choice, but their high sodium content and minimal protein and fiber make them a less than stellar snack. If you're reaching for a quick bite, don't be fooled by the marketing; you might want to look elsewhere for something more satisfying.
