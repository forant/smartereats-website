# Are Garden Veggie Straws Healthy? (Garden Veggie Straws vs Doritos)

Garden Veggie Straws often boast a health halo, marketed as a better-for-you snack option that’s light and veggie-based. However, peeling back the layers reveals that they may not be the upgrade you think. While they do contain vegetable powders, the nutritional profile raises some eyebrows, especially when compared to snacks like Doritos.

**Short answer:** Not really — Garden Veggie Straws may seem healthy, but their sodium and low protein content tell a different story.

## The quick verdict
Garden Veggie Straws scored 6/10. For context, Doritos also scored 6/10, making this a fair comparison. 

## What hurts Garden Veggie Straws
Garden Veggie Straws are high in sodium at 880 mg, which is quite significant for a light snack. Additionally, they barely contain any protein (0.21g) and fiber (0.29g), meaning they likely won't keep you feeling full for long. 

## Why the comparison matters
When comparing these two snacks, the similar scores reveal that Garden Veggie Straws, despite their marketing, may not offer a substantial advantage over Doritos. Both snacks carry the same score, but the high sodium content and lack of protein in Veggie Straws could be an issue for those mindful of their salt intake and looking for a filling snack.

## Bottom line
Garden Veggie Straws may not be worth the hype. If you're snacking, you might want to reconsider and look for options that provide more substantial nutrition rather than relying on these veggie straws that don't hold up as well as the marketing suggests.
