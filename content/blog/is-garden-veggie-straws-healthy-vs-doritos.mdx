# Is Garden Veggie Straws Healthy? (Garden Veggie Straws vs Doritos)

The health halo surrounding veggie snacks like Garden Veggie Straws can be misleading. They are often marketed as a healthier option, but the reality shows some significant tradeoffs compared to traditional snacks like Doritos.

## Quick Answer
Garden Veggie Straws score 6/10, making them a light snack option due to their lower calorie count of 72.95 per serving. However, their high sodium content and lack of protein and fiber limit their overall health benefits.

## Quick Verdict
- **Garden Veggie Straws:** 6/10
- **Doritos:** 6/10

Winner: neither — both snacks have shortcomings in nutrition despite their appealing marketing.

## Why Garden Veggie Straws Falls Short
- Contains 880 mg of sodium, which is quite high for a light snack.
- Lacks significant protein (0.21g) and fiber (0.29g), making it less filling.
- While it has vegetable powders like spinach and tomato, these don't significantly boost nutritional value.

## How Doritos Compares
- Doritos provide 10g of protein, which is high for a snack and promotes satiety.
- They contain 0g of total sugar, making them a low-sugar option.
- However, they are highly processed, and their moderate calorie density (150 calories per 28g serving) could lead to overeating.

## Best Choice Based on Your Goal
- **Weight loss:** neither — both options are lacking in nutritional quality.
- **Energy / satiety:** Doritos — higher protein content helps with fullness.
- **Occasional treat:** Doritos — more satisfying despite being processed.

## Better Alternatives
- Plain popcorn: a whole grain snack that is low in calories and high in fiber.
- Carrot sticks: crunchy and full of vitamins with low calories.
- Hummus and veggies: offers protein and fiber with a satisfying dip.
- Air-popped chips: lower in calories and fat compared to traditional chips.
- Greek yogurt: high in protein and can be a filling snack option.

Garden Veggie Straws, while lower in calories, fall short due to high sodium and minimal protein and fiber. With a score of 6/10, they do not provide a substantial health benefit. Opt for more nutrient-dense alternatives for better snack choices.
