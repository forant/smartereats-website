# Is Garden Veggie Straws Healthy? (Garden Veggie Straws vs Doritos)

Garden Veggie Straws often get a health halo, marketed as a better snack option thanks to their veggie appeal. However, when you dig into the numbers, this perception can be misleading. While they might seem like a guilt-free snack, a closer look reveals some significant trade-offs that challenge their healthy image.

**Short answer:** Not really — Garden Veggie Straws are low in calories but lack protein and fiber, making them less satisfying than you'd hope.

## The quick verdict
Garden Veggie Straws scored 6/10, which might sound decent, but it reveals a lack of substance. For reference, Doritos also scored 6/10.

## What hurts Garden Veggie Straws
- With 880 mg of sodium, it's quite high for a light snack, which could be a consideration for those monitoring salt intake.
- Lacks significant protein and fiber (0.21g and 0.29g respectively), which might not be very filling.

## Why the comparison matters
Both Garden Veggie Straws and Doritos scored the same, but the comparison sheds light on what’s really going on with these snacks. Garden Veggie Straws may be marketed as a healthier choice, but their sodium content and minimal protein/fiber leave much to be desired. While Doritos are undoubtedly processed, they offer more protein, which can help with satiety.

## Bottom line
Garden Veggie Straws don't live up to the healthy image they're often given. If you're looking for a snack that actually fills you up and delivers more nutrition, you might want to rethink reaching for those veggie straws.
