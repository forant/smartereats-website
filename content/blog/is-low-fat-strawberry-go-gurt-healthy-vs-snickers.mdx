# Is Low Fat Strawberry Go-Gurt Healthy? (Low Fat Strawberry Go-Gurt vs Snickers)

Parents often seek out healthy snacks for their kids, but not all marketed options live up to expectations. Low Fat Strawberry Go-Gurt may seem like a better choice than a Snickers bar, but the sugar content and lack of fiber tell a different story.

## Quick Answer
Low Fat Strawberry Go-Gurt scores 6/10, helped by 6g protein which is decent for a snack, providing some satiety. However, its 21g of sugar and zero fiber are significant drawbacks. In comparison, Snickers also scores 6/10, but with slightly more sugar at 24g.

## Quick Verdict
- **Low Fat Strawberry Go-Gurt:** 6/10
- **Snickers:** 6/10

Winner: neither — both options have high sugar content and low fiber.

## Why Low Fat Strawberry Go-Gurt Falls Short
- 21g of total sugar is relatively high for a snack, especially without added fiber.
- Very low fiber content (0g), which might not be as filling.
- Highly processed ingredients and added sugar lower ingredient quality.

## How Snickers Compares
- Snickers contains 24g of total sugar, slightly higher than Go-Gurt.
- It has 4g of protein, which is more than Go-Gurt, but still not ideal for a nutritious snack.
- Both snacks are highly processed with questionable ingredient quality.

## Best Choice Based on Your Goal
- **Weight loss:** neither — both are high in sugar and low in fiber.
- **Energy / satiety:** neither — both lack adequate nutritional density.
- **Occasional treat:** neither — both are overly processed and sugary.

## Better Alternatives
- Greek yogurt: higher in protein and lower in sugar.
- Fresh fruit: naturally sweet and packed with fiber.
- Hummus with veggies: nutritious and satisfying.
- Nuts: provide healthy fats and protein.
- Whole grain crackers: offer more fiber and less sugar.
