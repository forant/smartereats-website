# Is Plain Greek Yogurt Healthy?

## Quick Answer
Plain Greek Yogurt is a solid option depending on your dietary needs. It offers a high protein content that supports satiety, making it a good snack choice.

## Nutrition Snapshot
Plain Greek Yogurt has a balanced nutritional profile that makes it a favorable choice for many.  
- Calories per serving: Not specified  
- Protein: 15g  
- Sugar: 6g  
- Overall score: 8/10  

## What Makes Plain Greek Yogurt a Good Choice
- Plain Greek Yogurt provides 15g of protein, which is excellent for satiety in a snack.
- It has a low sugar content, just 6g of total sugar, making it less sweet and more balanced compared to flavored yogurts.
- Made with simple, cultured milk and live cultures.
- It's minimally processed and uses quality ingredients.

## Potential Downsides
- With 9g of total fat, including 5g saturated, Plain Greek Yogurt is richer than lower-fat yogurts.
- It might feel a bit heavy for some.
- The plain flavor might not appeal to everyone without adding a sweetener or fruit, which could increase calorie content.

## How Plain Greek Yogurt Fits Different Goals
- **Weight loss / calorie control:** A good fit if you prioritize protein and low sugar. However, watch portion sizes due to its fat content.
- **High protein / muscle support:** Excellent choice with 15g of protein, supporting muscle recovery and satiety.
- **Energy / satiety / blood sugar stability:** Good option for sustained energy due to its protein content, helping stabilize blood sugar levels.
- **Heart health / lower sodium / less processed eating:** Less ideal if you're concerned about saturated fat, with 5g per serving.

## Healthier or Better Alternatives
- Plain oatmeal: More fiber and less fat.
- Plain Greek yogurt with berries: Adds nutrients without much added sugar.
- Unsweetened almond milk: Lower in calories and fat compared to Plain Greek Yogurt.
- Hard-boiled egg: Higher in protein and lower in sugar.
- Cottage cheese: Similar protein content but often lower in fat.

## Final Verdict
Whether Plain Greek Yogurt is a good choice depends on your goal, portion size, and what you're comparing it to. It's a great option for those looking to increase protein intake and minimize sugar, but be cautious if you're monitoring fat content or prefer sweeter flavors.
