# Is Welch's Fruit Snacks Healthy? (Welch's Fruit Snacks vs Skittles)

Welch's Fruit Snacks often masquerade as a healthier treat, especially for kids, but the reality might be more revealing than the marketing suggests. With a high sugar content that could rival that of traditional candy, these snacks might not be the wholesome option parents hope for. In this comparison with Skittles, it becomes clear that the lines between fruit snacks and candy are blurrier than you’d expect.

**Short answer:** Not really — Welch's Fruit Snacks contain a hefty amount of sugar, making them less healthy than you'd think.

## The quick verdict
Welch's Fruit Snacks scored 5/10. In comparison, Skittles scored 4/10. This tells us that while Welch's may seem like a better option on the surface, it still falls short of being a truly healthy snack.

## What hurts Welch's Fruit Snacks
With 46.81g of total sugars, these fruit snacks are on the higher side, which could lead to a quick energy spike and crash. Calorie density is fairly high at 297.87 calories per 100g, making it easy to eat more than intended without feeling full. 

## Why the comparison matters
Skittles, with their 74g of total sugar and 400 calories per 100g, serve as a stark reminder that sweet snacks, whether marketed as fruit-based or not, can be deceptively similar. While Welch's Fruit Snacks may slightly edge out Skittles in terms of being lower in calories, their sugar content still invites skepticism about their health benefits.

## Bottom line
Welch's Fruit Snacks may be convenient for a sweet craving, especially for kids, but they shouldn't be mistaken for a nutritious choice. The high sugar content and calorie density make them a snack that’s more about immediate satisfaction than lasting energy or health benefits.
