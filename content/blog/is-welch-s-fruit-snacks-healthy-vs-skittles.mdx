# Are Welch's Fruit Snacks Healthy? (Welch's Fruit Snacks vs Skittles)

When it comes to kids' snacks, Welch's Fruit Snacks often wear a badge of healthiness, appealing to parents looking for something sweet yet nutritious. But the reality might be less rosy than the marketing suggests. With a staggering sugar content, these fruit snacks are closer to candy than you might think. This comparison with Skittles reveals just how much sugar is packed into these small bites.

**Short answer:** Not really — Welch's Fruit Snacks contain a high sugar load that could lead to an energy spike followed by a crash.

## The quick verdict
Welch's Fruit Snacks scored 5/10. In comparison, Skittles scored 4/10, highlighting that both options have significant drawbacks, but Welch's edges out slightly.

## What hurts Welch's Fruit Snacks
With 46.81g of total sugars, these fruit snacks are on the higher side, which could lead to a quick energy spike and crash. Additionally, calorie density is fairly high at 297.87 calories per 100g, making it easy to eat more than intended without feeling full. This sugar overload can easily transform a supposedly healthier snack into an energy rollercoaster.

## Why the comparison matters
Skittles, while slightly lower in score, pack a whopping 74g of total sugars per 100g, and lack protein or fiber, which makes them more of an indulgence than a substantial snack. While Welch's Fruit Snacks do contain some protein (4.26g) that might help with satiety, their high sugar content showcases that they aren't as healthy as they seem. This comparison reveals that both snacks falter when it comes to providing balanced nutrition.

## Bottom line
Welch's Fruit Snacks may seem like a better choice, but their high sugar content and calorie density mean they should be approached with caution. Parents looking for a snack for their kids should consider the hidden downsides of these fruit snacks before reaching for the package.
