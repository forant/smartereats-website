# Is Welch's Fruit Snacks Healthy? (Welch's Fruit Snacks vs Skittles)

When it comes to kids' snacks, Welch's Fruit Snacks often wear a halo of healthiness, marketed as a fruity alternative to traditional candy. But how do they really stack up? This comparison with Skittles reveals that the sugar content in Welch's Fruit Snacks might be more alarming than you'd expect, despite their fruit-forward branding.

**Short answer:** Not really — Welch's Fruit Snacks have a high sugar load that could lead to quick energy spikes and crashes.

## The quick verdict
Welch's Fruit Snacks scored 5/10. For context, Skittles scored 4/10.

## What hurts Welch's Fruit Snacks
With 46.81g of total sugars, these fruit snacks are on the higher side, which could lead to a quick energy spike and crash. Additionally, calorie density is fairly high at 297.87 calories per 100g, making it easy to eat more than intended without feeling full.

## Why the comparison matters
Skittles, while slightly lower on the scorecard, provide a clear benchmark for understanding Welch's Fruit Snacks. The fact that Skittles have 74g of total sugars and a calorie density of 400 calories per 100g shows that Welch's Fruit Snacks, despite their fruit branding, are not drastically better in terms of sugar content and can be just as calorie-dense. 

## Bottom line
Welch's Fruit Snacks may seem like a healthier choice, but their high sugar content and calorie density indicate they are not as beneficial as they appear. If you’re looking for a snack that will keep you full and energized without the sugar crash, you might want to consider other options.
