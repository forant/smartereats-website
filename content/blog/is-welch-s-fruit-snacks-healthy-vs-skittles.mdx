# Is Welch's Fruit Snacks Healthy? (Welch's Fruit Snacks vs Skittles)

Parents often wonder if Welch's Fruit Snacks are a healthier option for their kids compared to Skittles. Both are popular snacks but come with different nutritional profiles. Understanding these differences can help make informed choices.

## Quick Answer
Welch's Fruit Snacks has a score of 5/10, indicating that while they are low in fat and contain some protein, their high sugar content of 46.81g can lead to energy spikes and crashes. In contrast, Skittles scores 4/10 and has even more sugar at 74g, but lacks protein or fiber, resulting in empty calories. 

## Quick Verdict
- **Welch's Fruit Snacks**: 5/10
- **Skittles**: 4/10

Winner: Welch's Fruit Snacks — they offer slightly more nutritional value with some protein content.

## Why Welch's Fruit Snacks Falls Short
- With 46.81g of total sugars, these fruit snacks are on the higher side, which could lead to a quick energy spike and crash.
- Calorie density is fairly high at 297.87 calories per 100g, making it easy to eat more than intended without feeling full.
- Sugar overload could contribute to potential long-term health concerns if consumed frequently.

## How Skittles Compares
- Skittles provide a quick burst of energy due to their 74g of total sugar, which can be refreshing as a light snack, but this also means they are even sweeter.
- They have no protein or fiber, which means they won't offer much satiety or balance as a snack.
- While Skittles are calorie-dense at 400 calories per 100g, they are lower in sodium, containing only 10mg, making them a low-salt option.

## Best Choice Based on Your Goal
- **Weight loss:** neither — pick a real alternative.
- **Energy / satiety:** Welch's Fruit Snacks — they have some protein to help with satiety.
- **Occasional treat:** neither — pick a real alternative.
