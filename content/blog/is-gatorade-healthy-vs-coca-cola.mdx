# Is GATORADE Healthy? (GATORADE vs Coca-Cola)

GATORADE often struts around with a reputation as a go-to sports drink, but how healthy is it really? While it’s marketed as a hydration powerhouse, the reality is that it packs a sugar punch without much else to offer. This comparison with Coca-Cola reveals just how similar these two drinks are in terms of nutritional value.

**Short answer:** Not really — GATORADE's sugar overload and lack of protein make it a poor choice for a satisfying snack.

## The quick verdict
GATORADE scored 5/10. In comparison, Coca-Cola scored 4/10. While GATORADE might seem like the better option, both drinks fall short when it comes to nutrition.

## What hurts GATORADE
GATORADE lacks protein or fiber, which means it won't be very filling or satisfying as a snack. It contains 8.4g of total sugar, typical for a beverage like GATORADE, but not ideal for a snack. These downsides overshadow its benefits, which include being low in calories at 48 per serving and providing 110 mg of sodium, potentially helpful after exercise or on a hot day.

## Why the comparison matters
Coca-Cola’s score of 4/10 reveals that it’s not much better than GATORADE when it comes to actual nutritional value. Both drinks share a similar profile, with high sugar content and minimal protein or fiber, indicating that choosing GATORADE over Coca-Cola isn't as significant a step up as you might think. You’re not making a better choice.

## Bottom line
GATORADE may claim to be a hydrating sports drink, but it doesn't deliver on essential nutrients for a satisfying snack. If you're looking for something that actually fuels you, it's best to steer clear of both GATORADE and Coca-Cola.
