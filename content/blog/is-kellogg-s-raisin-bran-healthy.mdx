# Is Kellogg's Raisin Bran Healthy?

## Quick Answer
Kellogg's Raisin Bran can be a relatively healthy breakfast option depending on your dietary goals. It provides a good balance of protein and fiber, but you'll want to consider its sugar content.

## Nutrition Snapshot
Kellogg's Raisin Bran scores 7/10, indicating it is a strong breakfast option with minor tradeoffs. It provides a decent amount of satiety for a cereal.

- Calories per serving: Not specified
- Protein: 5g
- Fiber: 7g
- Sugar: 17g
- Sodium: Not specified
- Overall score: 7/10

## What Makes Kellogg's Raisin Bran a Good Choice
- Kellogg's Raisin Bran provides 5g of protein, offering a decent amount of satiety for a cereal.
- With 7g of fiber, it can help keep you full for a longer period compared to many other cereals.
- The whole grain wheat and raisins add some nutritional quality, supporting its role as a balanced breakfast choice.

## Potential Downsides
- The 17g of total sugar is on the higher side, which might be something to keep in mind if sugar intake is a concern.
- While it is a cereal and inherently processed, the inclusion of sugar and brown sugar syrup could be something to be mindful of for those preferring less processed options.

## How Kellogg's Raisin Bran Fits Different Goals
- **Weight loss / calorie control:** Less ideal if you're watching sugar intake due to its 17g of sugar per serving.
- **High protein / muscle support:** Good fit if you're looking for a moderate protein boost with 5g per serving.
- **Energy / satiety / blood sugar stability:** Good fit if you need sustained energy, thanks to 7g of fiber, which can help maintain fullness.
- **Heart health / lower sodium / less processed eating:** Less ideal if you prefer less processed foods, as it contains added sugars and is inherently processed.

## Healthier or Better Alternatives
- Plain oatmeal: More filling per calorie and less processed.
- Plain Greek yogurt: Higher in protein and lower in sugar.
- Whole fruit (like a banana or apple): Less added sugar and more vitamins.
- Hard-boiled eggs: Higher in protein and less processed.
- Unsweetened almond milk: Lower in calories and sugar than Kellogg's Raisin Bran.

## Final Verdict
Whether Kellogg's Raisin Bran is a good choice depends on your goal, portion size, and what you're comparing it to. It can be a solid option for those needing a filling breakfast with some protein and fiber, but those concerned about sugar content should approach it with caution.
