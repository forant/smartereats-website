# Is Nature Valley Peanut Butter Chocolate Granola Healthy? (Nature Valley Peanut Butter Chocolate Granola vs Snickers)

Many consumers believe that granola snacks are healthier than candy bars. Nature Valley Peanut Butter Chocolate Granola positions itself as a nutritious choice, but how does it stack up against a familiar candy like Snickers? The comparison reveals some important insights into its nutritional profile.

## Quick Answer
Nature Valley Peanut Butter Chocolate Granola scores 7/10, thanks to its 3g of protein, which can help with satiety compared to many other snack options. However, with 9g of total sugars, it’s important to consider its sweetness relative to its health halo.

## Quick Verdict
- **Nature Valley Peanut Butter Chocolate Granola:** 7/10
- **Snickers:** 6/10

Winner: Nature Valley Peanut Butter Chocolate Granola — it offers more protein and fiber, though both snacks have high sugar content.

## Why Nature Valley Peanut Butter Chocolate Granola Falls Short
- Contains 9g of total sugars, which is considerable for a small portion, making it less ideal for those monitoring sugar intake.
- With 4.5g of saturated fat, it's higher in this type of fat than some other snacks.
- Only provides 1g of fiber, which is relatively low for a snack aiming for satiety.

## How Snickers Compares
- Snickers contains 24g of total sugars, which is significantly higher than Nature Valley, leading to energy spikes.
- Provides 4g of protein, slightly more than Nature Valley, but still less than ideal for a healthy snack.
- Known for its processed ingredients, including artificial flavors and corn syrup, which may not appeal to health-conscious consumers.

## Best Choice Based on Your Goal
- **Weight loss:** neither — both have high sugar levels.
- **Energy / satiety:** Nature Valley Peanut Butter Chocolate Granola — offers more protein.
- **Occasional treat:** Snickers — more enjoyable flavor but much higher in sugar.

## Better Alternatives
- Plain Greek yogurt: High in protein and low in sugar.
- Fresh fruit: Natural sugars with fiber for satiety.
- Raw nuts: Healthy fats and protein without added sugars.
- Air-popped popcorn: A low-calorie, high-fiber snack.
- Dark chocolate: Offers antioxidants with less sugar than candy bars.
