# Is Kellogg's Raisin Bran Healthy? (Kellogg's Raisin Bran vs Frosted Flakes)

## Quick Answer
Kellogg's Raisin Bran is a relatively healthy choice, scoring 7/10, but it has notable sugar content. Compared to Frosted Flakes, it provides more protein and fiber.

## Quick Verdict
- Kellogg's Raisin Bran: 7/10
- Frosted Flakes: 6/10

Winner: Kellogg's Raisin Bran — it offers better nutritional benefits with protein and fiber.

## Why Kellogg's Raisin Bran Falls Short
- Contains 17g of total sugar, which is relatively high without clear labeling of added sugars.
- Includes processed sugars like sugar and brown sugar syrup, which can be a concern.
- While it provides 5g of protein and 7g of fiber, it may not keep you full for long due to lower fat content.
- Reasonable calorie content at 190 calories, but it may need additional ingredients to enhance satiety.

## How Frosted Flakes Compares
- Frosted Flakes offers a quick, convenient option with only 137 calories per serving.
- However, it has 12.65g of sugar, which may not provide sustained energy.
- Lacks protein, with only 2.11g, making it less filling than Kellogg's Raisin Bran.

## Best Choice Based on Your Goal
- **Weight loss:** Kellogg's Raisin Bran — slightly lower in sugar and more filling.
- **Energy / satiety:** Kellogg's Raisin Bran — higher protein and fiber content.
- **Occasional treat:** Frosted Flakes — suitable for a quick, sweet breakfast.

## Better Alternatives
- Plain Greek yogurt — high in protein and low in sugar.
- Oatmeal — a whole grain option that can be customized with fruits and nuts.
- Hard-boiled eggs — a protein-rich, low-carb breakfast option.
- Fresh fruit — natural sugars and fiber without processed ingredients.
- Unsweetened almond milk — a low-calorie alternative for cereal.

## Bottom Line
Kellogg's Raisin Bran is a stronger breakfast option compared to Frosted Flakes, thanks to its higher protein and fiber content. However, be cautious of its sugar levels. Consider adding nuts or fruits to enhance its nutritional profile.
