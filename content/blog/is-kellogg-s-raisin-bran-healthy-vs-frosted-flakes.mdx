# Is Kellogg's Raisin Bran Healthy? (Kellogg's Raisin Bran vs Frosted Flakes)

Consumers often perceive Kellogg's Raisin Bran as a healthier breakfast option due to its marketing. However, the reality is more nuanced when compared to Frosted Flakes. Understanding the nutritional differences is crucial for making informed breakfast choices.

## Quick Answer
Kellogg's Raisin Bran scores 7/10, helped by providing 5g of protein and 7g of fiber which can contribute to satiety. In contrast, Frosted Flakes scores 6/10 and lacks significant protein, leading to potentially less fullness.

## Quick Verdict
- **Kellogg's Raisin Bran**: 7/10
- **Frosted Flakes**: 6/10

Winner: Kellogg's Raisin Bran — it offers better protein and fiber content, contributing to satiety.

## Why Kellogg's Raisin Bran Falls Short
- Contains **17g of total sugar**, which is relatively high for a breakfast cereal.
- Includes processed sugars like sugar and brown sugar syrup, raising concerns about added sugars.
- May not provide long-lasting fullness on its own due to lower fat content.

## How Frosted Flakes Compares
- Frosted Flakes has **12.65g of sugar**, making it quite sweet, yet still less than Raisin Bran.
- Lacks protein with only **2.11g**, making it less filling compared to Kellogg's Raisin Bran.
- While lower in calories at **137 per serving**, it may not sustain energy levels or satiety throughout the morning.

## Best Choice Based on Your Goal
- **Weight loss:** Kellogg's Raisin Bran — better balance of protein and fiber.
- **Energy / satiety:** Kellogg's Raisin Bran — offers more nutrients for fullness.
- **Occasional treat:** Neither — pick a real alternative.

## Better Alternatives
- Plain Greek yogurt: High in protein and low in sugar.
- Oatmeal: Whole grains with fiber and minimal sugar.
- Scrambled eggs: High in protein and satisfying.
- Fresh fruit: Nutrient-rich with natural sweetness and fiber.
- Unsweetened almond milk: Low calorie and versatile for breakfast.

## Bottom Line
Kellogg's Raisin Bran offers a better score than Frosted Flakes due to its protein and fiber content, but the sugar level is a concern. For a healthy breakfast, consider options like plain Greek yogurt or oatmeal, which provide more nutrients and less sugar.
