# Is Triscuits Healthy? (Triscuits vs Doritos)

Many people consider Triscuits a healthier snack option due to their marketing as whole grain products. However, when compared to Doritos, the differences in nutrition may not support this health halo. Understanding the nutritional profiles of these snacks shows a more nuanced picture.

## Quick Answer
Triscuits score 7/10, with 3g of fiber per serving that may help with satiety. However, they only provide 3g of protein and include some added sugars. Doritos also score 7/10 but offer 10g of protein and have lower sugar content, making them a more satisfying choice.

## Quick Verdict
- **Triscuits:** 7/10
- **Doritos:** 7/10

Winner: neither — both snacks have similar scores, but Doritos provide more protein.

## Why Triscuits Falls Short
- This snack only provides **3g of protein**, which is modest and might not keep you full for very long.
- Though not high, the **1g of sugar** includes an unspecified amount of added sugar, which might not be ideal if consumed frequently.
- Includes some level of processing with added oils and flavorings, though typical for crackers.

## How Doritos Compares
- **10g protein per serving** supports satiety, making them more filling than Triscuits.
- **0g total sugars** can be favorable for a snack, helping to avoid excess sugar intake.
- However, they are highly processed with ingredients like vegetable oils and corn syrup solids, which some may prefer to avoid.

## Best Choice Based on Your Goal
- **Weight loss:** neither — both options have similar calorie counts but lack substantial nutritional benefits.
- **Energy / satiety:** Doritos — higher protein content makes them more filling.
- **Occasional treat:** neither — consider alternatives for healthier snacking.

## Better Alternatives
- Plain Greek yogurt — high in protein and low in sugar.
- Mixed nuts — provide healthy fats and protein.
- Air-popped popcorn — a whole grain, low-calorie snack option.
- Fresh fruit — naturally sweet and full of fiber.
- Hummus with veggies — nutritious and filling with protein and fiber.
