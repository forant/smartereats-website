Is Pop Corners Healthy? (Pop Corners vs Doritos)

Quick Answer

Pop Corners is a reasonably balanced snack with a score of 7/10, while Doritos scores slightly lower at 6/10. Pop Corners provide 140 calories and a better sodium profile, but lack fiber for sustained fullness.

Quick Verdict

  • Pop Corners: 7/10
  • Doritos: 6/10

Winner: Pop Corners — it offers fewer calories and a healthier sodium level.

Why Pop Corners Falls Short

  • Zero fiber content may limit sustained fullness compared to snacks with more fiber.
  • Processing and ingredient list could be longer, with more additives than simple whole foods.
  • Contains 6g of fat, which may add up if not mindful, but it's not excessive for snack chips.

How Doritos Compares

  • Doritos contain a solid 10g of protein, which is higher than typical for chips and supports satiety.
  • While Doritos are higher in calories at 150, they have low sugar content at 0g, making them suitable for avoiding added sugars.
  • However, they are highly processed with many ingredients, which some might prefer to avoid in everyday snacks.

Best Choice Based on Your Goal

  • Weight loss: Pop Corners — lower in calories.
  • Energy / satiety: Neither — both lack sufficient fiber.
  • Occasional treat: Pop Corners — offers more balanced nutrition.

Better Alternatives

  • Plain popcorn for a whole grain option with fiber.
  • Air-popped rice cakes for a light, low-calorie snack.
  • Celery sticks with hummus for a crunchy, nutritious choice.
  • Baby carrots for a fiber-rich snack.
  • Unsweetened Greek yogurt for protein and probiotics.

Bottom Line

Pop Corners is a solid snack choice with lower calories and better sodium levels than Doritos. However, both snacks fall short in fiber, limiting their ability to keep you full. Choose wisely if you're looking for a nutritious snack.
