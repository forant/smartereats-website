Is Cheez-Its Healthy? (Cheez-Its vs Pringles)

Quick Answer

Cheez-Its score 6/10, indicating they are a light snack but not very filling. Compared to Pringles, they have a similar score but offer less protein and satiety.

Quick Verdict

  • Cheez-Its: 6/10
  • Pringles: 6/10

Winner: neither — both are ultra-processed snacks with minimal nutritional benefits.

Why Cheez-Its Falls Short

  • Very low protein content at 0.81g, which may not offer much satiety.
  • Light snack with 40.5 calories, which can tide someone over without being too heavy.
  • Ingredient quality and level of processing are unknown, possibly less wholesome compared to less processed snacks.
  • Ultra-processed junk.

How Pringles Compares

  • Similar to Cheez-Its, Pringles are also ultra-processed junk.
  • Pringles contain 1g of fiber, which can help with satiety as a light snack.
  • High total fat content of 7g in a small serving may lead to overeating if not careful.

Best Choice Based on Your Goal

  • Weight loss: neither — pick a real alternative.
  • Energy / satiety: neither — both are low in protein and filling.
  • Occasional treat: neither — both are ultra-processed snacks.

Better Alternatives

  • Plain popcorn: a whole grain snack that's light and satisfying.
  • Fresh fruit (like an apple): naturally sweet and hydrating.
  • Nuts (like almonds): provide healthy fats and protein for satiety.
  • Plain Greek yogurt: high in protein and can keep you full longer.
  • Vegetable sticks with hummus: crunchy, nutritious, and filling.

Bottom Line

Cheez-Its are not a healthy snack choice. They lack protein and are highly processed, similar to Pringles. Consider more nutritious alternatives for a satisfying snack.
