Is Gogurt Healthy? (Gogurt vs Snickers)

Quick Answer

Gogurt is not a particularly healthy choice, scoring 6/10. While it has fewer calories than Snickers, its sugar content is still concerning.

Quick Verdict

  • Gogurt: 6/10
  • Snickers: 6/10

Winner: neither — both have high sugar content and are processed.

Why Gogurt Falls Short

  • 7.25g total sugar may be on the higher side without fiber to offset fullness.
  • Liquid form might not satisfy hunger as effectively as solid snacks.
  • Ultra-processed junk with potentially unspecified ingredient quality.

How Snickers Compares

  • Snickers has a higher sugar content at 24g, significantly more than Gogurt.
  • Both snacks are ultra-processed, which raises concerns about ingredient quality.
  • Snickers provides 4g of protein for satiety, slightly more than Gogurt's 1.98g.

Best Choice Based on Your Goal

  • Weight loss: neither — both options are high in sugar and low in nutritional value.
  • Energy / satiety: neither — neither provides lasting energy or fullness.
  • Occasional treat: neither — both are processed snacks that should be limited.

Better Alternatives

  • Plain Greek yogurt: higher in protein and lower in sugar.
  • Fresh fruit: naturally sweet, full of fiber and vitamins.
  • Nuts: provide healthy fats and protein for satiety.
  • Hard-boiled egg: packed with protein and nutrients.
  • Unsweetened applesauce: lower in sugar and more filling.

Bottom Line

Gogurt does not stand out as a healthy snack option. Its high sugar content, combined with being ultra-processed, makes it less desirable. Consider fresh, whole foods for better nutrition.
