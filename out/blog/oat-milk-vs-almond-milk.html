Oat Milk vs Almond Milk: Which Is Healthier?

Oat milk is creamier; almond milk has fewer calories. But the real differences are in protein, sugar, and how each one is actually made.

Plant milks are a category where the marketing has badly outrun the nutrition. "Oat milk" sounds like a glass of liquid oats. "Almond milk" sounds like blended almonds. Neither is quite accurate — and the gap between the two on a label is bigger than most people realize.

The lineup

We're comparing the most common formulations on US shelves, per 240 ml (1 cup):

  • Oatly Original Oat Milk
  • Almond Breeze Original Almond Milk (sweetened)
  • Almond Breeze Unsweetened Almond Milk

Side-by-side

| Metric | Oat (Oatly) | Almond (sweet) | Almond (unsweet) | | -------------- | ----------- | -------------- | ---------------- | | Calories | 120 | 60 | 30 | | Total fat | 5 g | 2.5 g | 2.5 g | | Total carbs | 16 g | 8 g | 1 g | | Total sugars | 7 g | 7 g | 0 g | | Added sugars | 0 g | 7 g | 0 g | | Protein | 3 g | 1 g | 1 g | | Calcium | 350 mg | 450 mg | 450 mg |

What's actually in each one

Oat milk is roughly 10% oats by weight. Oats are mixed with water and enzymes that break starch into smaller sugars (mostly maltose), then strained. That enzymatic step is why oat milk tastes mildly sweet and shows ~7 g of total sugar even with zero added sugar on the label — those sugars come from the oats themselves.

Almond milk is even more dilute — usually 2% almonds by weight. The rest is water, thickeners (gellan gum, locust bean gum), salt, and added vitamins. That's why the unsweetened version drops to 30 calories: there's barely any almond in it.

What actually matters

Sugar reads. Sweetened almond milk hides 7 g of added sugar in an "almond" product. Oat milk's 7 g comes from the oats themselves and isn't classified as added — but your body doesn't know the difference, and you'll see a similar glycemic response.

Protein. Both are bad at this. Oat: 3 g. Almond: 1 g. Cow's milk has 8 g for comparison. If you're using plant milk in coffee, fine. If you're using it as a milk replacement in cereal expecting to feel full, you'll need protein elsewhere.

Fortified calcium does most of the calcium delivery in both — it's added during processing, not naturally present.

Processing. Both are highly processed. Marketing leans on the "just oats and water" framing, but enzymes, oils, gums, and stabilizers are usually in the mix. That's not automatically bad — but it's not the whole-food image the carton implies.

The verdict

There's no single "healthier" — it depends on what you're optimizing for:

  • Lowest calories / lowest sugar: unsweetened almond milk wins clearly.
  • Best texture in coffee, more protein, no added sugar: oat milk.
  • Most almond-y: none of these. If you actually want almonds, eat almonds.
  • Avoid: sweetened almond milk. You're paying for almond marketing and getting a sugar-water profile.

The insight most people miss

Plant milks are not nutritionally similar to cow's milk, and they're not nutritionally similar to each other. Picking between them isn't about which is "clean" — it's about which trade-off (calories, protein, mouthfeel, sugar) you actually care about for the use case at hand.

A reasonable default for most people: oat milk for coffee, unsweetened almond for cereal, real food for actual nutrition.
