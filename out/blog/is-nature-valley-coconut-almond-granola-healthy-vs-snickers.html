Is Nature Valley Coconut Almond Granola Healthy? (Nature Valley Coconut Almond Granola vs Snickers)

Many snack options claim to be healthier, but how does Nature Valley Coconut Almond Granola hold up against Snickers? With its marketing focusing on wholesome ingredients, it’s essential to look closely at the nutritional aspects to see what this granola really delivers.

Quick Answer

Nature Valley Coconut Almond Granola scores 6/10, helped by containing 9.38g of protein, which is substantial for a granola and can help with satiety. However, it also has high sugar and calorie content that could lead to energy spikes and cravings.

Quick Verdict

  • Nature Valley Coconut Almond Granola: 6/10
  • Snickers: 6/10

Winner: neither — both options have significant sugar and calorie concerns.

Why Nature Valley Coconut Almond Granola Falls Short

  • Contains 437.5 calories per 100g serving, making it calorie-dense and requiring portion control.
  • Has 26.56g of total sugars, which is quite high for a snack and could affect energy levels and cravings.
  • Considered "empty calories," providing less nutritional value relative to caloric intake.
  • While it contains high-quality ingredients like whole grain oats and almonds, the sugar content overshadows these benefits.

How Snickers Compares

  • Snickers contains 24g of total sugar, which is also high but slightly less than the granola.
  • At 210 calories, it is less calorie-dense than the granola, making it easier to manage portions.
  • Both snacks are processed, but Snickers includes artificial flavors, contributing to its classification as junk food.

Best Choice Based on Your Goal

  • Weight loss: neither — both options are calorie-dense and high in sugar.
  • Energy / satiety: neither — both can lead to energy spikes followed by crashes.
  • Occasional treat: neither — both should be consumed sparingly due to their sugar content.

Better Alternatives

  • Plain Greek yogurt: High in protein and lower in sugar.
  • Fresh fruit: Natural sugars with added fiber and vitamins.
  • Raw nuts: Healthy fats and protein, no added sugars.
  • Air-popped popcorn: Low-calorie and satisfying without added sugars.
  • Dark chocolate: Offers antioxidants with less sugar than typical snacks.

Bottom Line

Nature Valley Coconut Almond Granola is not a healthy snack option due to its high sugar and calorie content. With a score of 6/10, it is important to be cautious about portion sizes. Consider healthier alternatives like plain Greek yogurt or fresh fruit for a more nutritious choice.
