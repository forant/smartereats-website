Is Garden Veggie Straws Sour Cream & Chives Healthy? (Garden Veggie Straws Sour Cream & Chives vs Doritos)

Many people consider Garden Veggie Straws Sour Cream & Chives a healthier snack option, but how does it really stack up against Doritos? While marketed as a light snack, the nutritional profile shows some surprising gaps.

Quick Answer

Garden Veggie Straws Sour Cream & Chives scores a 5/10, indicating it's a fairly low-protein and low-fiber choice that may not satisfy hunger effectively. In comparison, Doritos score a 6/10, offering more protein, but they're also more processed.

Quick Verdict

  • Garden Veggie Straws Sour Cream & Chives: 5/10
  • Doritos: 6/10

Winner: neither — both snacks have their downsides, but Veggie Straws lack protein and fiber.

Why Garden Veggie Straws Sour Cream & Chives Falls Short

  • Barely any protein: With only 0.4g, these straws may leave you feeling hungry shortly after eating.
  • Low fiber: Also at 0.4g, they're not a great option for promoting fullness.
  • Saturated fat concerns: Total fat is 3.2g, with all of it being saturated fat, which is important to consider for heart health.
  • Moderate sodium: At 90mg, it's lower than many savory snacks but still noteworthy.

How Doritos Compares

  • Higher protein content: Doritos provide 10g of protein, which is significantly more than Veggie Straws, aiding in satiety.
  • Moderate calorie count: At 150 calories per serving, Doritos can serve as a light snack option, albeit not the healthiest.
  • More processed: Doritos have a long ingredient list and are made with multiple oils, which may deter those looking for whole food snacks.

Best Choice Based on Your Goal

  • Weight loss: neither — both snacks are low in nutrients that promote satiety.
  • Energy / satiety: Doritos — the higher protein content may help you feel fuller longer.
  • Occasional treat: neither — choose a more nutrient-dense option for an occasional indulgence.

Better Alternatives

  • Plain Greek yogurt — high in protein and filling.
  • Hummus with veggies — nutritious and satisfying.
  • Air-popped popcorn — whole grain and low-calorie.
  • Fresh fruit — naturally sweet and hydrating.
  • Nuts — healthy fats and protein for satiety.

Garden Veggie Straws Sour Cream & Chives fall short due to their low protein and fiber content, scoring only 5/10. For a snack that provides more satiety, consider alternatives like Greek yogurt or hummus.
